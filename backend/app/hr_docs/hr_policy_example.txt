Standard Chartered Bank - HR Policy Handbook
==============================================

1. Annual Leave Policy
Standard Chartered Bank provides employees with 22 working days of annual leave per calendar year, pro-rated for joiners and leavers. Unused leave may be carried forward up to 5 days with manager approval. Leave requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance. All employees are encouraged to utilise their full leave entitlement to support work-life balance.

2. Sick Leave & Medical Benefits
Employees are entitled to 14 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 2 consecutive days. SCB provides comprehensive medical insurance covering hospitalisation, outpatient care, and dental treatment. Coverage extends to spouse and dependent children. Employees may also access SCB's Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) for mental health support, available 24/7.

3. Parental Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of fully paid maternity leave. Secondary caregivers receive 4 weeks of fully paid paternity leave. Adoption leave is also available at the same entitlement. Flexible return-to-work options are available for all parents, including phased return over 4 weeks.

4. Work Hours & Flexible Working
Standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday to Friday, with a 1-hour lunch break. SCB supports hybrid working arrangements: employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week, subject to manager approval and business requirements. Core hours (10:00 AM to 4:00 PM) require all employees to be available. Flexible start and end times are available between 7:30 AM and 9:30 AM (start) and 4:30 PM to 6:30 PM (end).

5. Code of Conduct & Ethics
As a Standard Chartered Bank employee, you must adhere to the SCB Code of Conduct which prohibits bribery, corruption, insider trading, and money laundering. All employees must complete the annual mandatory Code of Conduct training. Conflicts of interest must be declared. Gifts and entertainment from clients or vendors must not exceed USD 100 in value without prior approval. SCB has a zero-tolerance policy towards harassment, discrimination, and workplace bullying.

6. Performance Management
SCB follows a semi-annual performance review cycle with reviews in March and September. Employees are evaluated on both "What" (business goals) and "How" (behaviours aligned with SCB values: Do the right thing, Never settle, Better together). Ratings range from 1 (Exceptional) to 5 (Unsatisfactory). A performance improvement plan (PIP) is initiated for employees rated 4 or below. Promotions are considered during the annual talent review in April.

7. Compensation & Benefits
SCB offers a competitive total compensation package including base salary, annual performance bonus (paid in March), and restricted share units for eligible employees. Benefits include private medical insurance, life insurance (4x annual salary), pension contributions at 10% of base salary, and a flexible benefits allowance that can be used for additional leave, gym memberships, or wellness programmes.

8. Learning & Development
Every employee has access to SCB's Learning Hub with over 5,000 online courses. A minimum of 40 learning hours per year is expected. Employees have an annual professional development budget of USD 3,000 for external certifications and courses. SCB offers a global mobility programme allowing employees to apply for international assignments and secondments across our markets.

9. Diversity & Inclusion
SCB is committed to building a diverse and inclusive workplace. Our Women in Leadership initiative targets 35% female representation in senior management by 2025. Employee resource groups include Pride (LGBTQ+), Women in Tech, and Cultural Diversity Network. All hiring panels must include diverse representation. Unconscious bias training is mandatory for all managers.

10. Travel & Expenses
Business class travel is authorised for international flights exceeding 6 hours. Economy class is standard for flights under 6 hours. Daily subsistence allowance varies by location: USD 100 for most markets. All travel must be booked through the SCB corporate travel portal. Expense claims must be submitted within 30 days with original receipts.

11. Notice Period & Resignation
Notice period for employees is 30 days for staff up to manager level, 60 days for senior managers and above. Garden leave may be applied at the bank's discretion. Upon resignation, a clearance process must be completed including return of all bank property, laptop, access cards, and confidential materials. Full and final settlement is processed within 30 days of the last working day.

12. Dress Code
SCB maintains a professional dress code. Business formal attire is expected when meeting clients or external stakeholders. Business casual is permitted on regular office days. Casual dress (jeans and t-shirts) is allowed on Fridays. National dress is welcome and encouraged.
